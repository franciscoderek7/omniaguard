## SECTION 2 — DOCUMENTED INCIDENTS

The following events are documented in contemporaneous records, recordings made pursuant to s.184(2)(a) of the *Criminal Code of Canada*, and written communications. All records have been preserved.

**Incident 1 — In-Store: Norman (Sales Representative)**

I visited a Kudos retail location while managing a documented disability. The in-store experience resulted in three separate violations:

**(a) Hookup Fees on Free Devices:** Norman charged **$490.00 in hookup fees** on devices that were represented to me as free of charge. Kudos's own records will confirm that these devices were part of a promotion under which hookup fees should not have been applied. This charge was unauthorized and is contested in its entirety.

**(b) Policy Violation — Watches Without Plans:** Norman provided watches to my account without an active plan — a direct violation of Kudos's own internal policy. This action created downstream billing and account complications that I have been left to resolve.

**(c) Disabled Customer Made to Travel:** Despite my disclosed disability, Norman required me to make a physical trip to the mall to address a problem that originated with Kudos. Requiring a disabled customer to physically attend a retail location to resolve a company-caused billing error, when the same resolution could have been provided remotely, is an accommodation failure under the *Ontario Human Rights Code*, R.S.O. 1990, c. H.19.

---

**Incident 2 — Price Misrepresentation**

During the sales process, devices and plans were quoted to me at **$10.00 per month**. My actual bill was **$70.00 per month** — a difference of **$60.00 per month** per line.

I explicitly told the representative that I could not afford $70.00 per month. Despite this, the contract was structured at the higher rate. Under *Queen v. Cognos Inc.* [1993] 1 SCR 87, a negligent — or fraudulent — misrepresentation made during a pre-contractual sales process, where the plaintiff relies on the represented figure, is actionable. I relied on the $10.00 quoted figure in entering this arrangement.

Under *Queen v. Cognos*, supra, and settled principles of vicarious liability, Kudos is bound by these admissions.
