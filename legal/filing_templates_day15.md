**Subject:** False/Misleading Representations and Bait-and-Switch Selling — CIBC ATM Referral Advertisement

**Description of Complaint:**

CIBC ATMs display "Refer a friend, get $75" to an estimated 11 million customers across Canada. The advertisement does not disclose a material condition: the referred friend must make a minimum deposit of $500 within a specified period.

**Section 52 (Criminal):** This omission constitutes a representation to the public that is false or misleading in a material respect. The $500 deposit requirement is a material condition that would affect a reasonable customer's decision to participate in the referral program. Failure to disclose it at the point of advertisement constitutes an offence under s.52 of the *Competition Act*, RSC 1985, c C-34.

**Section 74.04 (Bait-and-Switch):** CIBC advertised a $75 bonus and then denied it based on a condition ($500 deposit) not disclosed in the ATM advertisement. This is bait-and-switch selling under s.74.04.

**Section 74.1 (Drip Pricing — 2024 Amendments):** The $500 deposit condition is a material hidden element of the offer not disclosed in the initial advertisement, engaging the 2024 drip pricing provisions.

**Scale of Harm:**
- CIBC has approximately 11 million customers
- 1% participation estimate = 110,000 affected customers
- $75 bonus per customer = approximately $8.25M in undisclosed exposure
- If total fraud exceeds $1M, mandatory minimum sentencing provisions under s.380(1.1) of the *Criminal Code* may apply

**Complainant's Personal Loss:**
Mr. Francisco referred John Ma Fusco in reliance on the ATM advertisement. After 150 days, the referral bonus was denied due to the undisclosed deposit condition. The referral damaged Mr. Francisco's relationship with his friend.

**Evidence Available:**
- Photograph of CIBC ATM advertisement (complainant to attach)
- Correspondence with CIBC regarding denial of referral bonus
- CIBC's written or verbal explanation of the $500 deposit requirement

**Relief Requested:**
- Investigation of CIBC's ATM referral advertising program
- Order requiring immediate disclosure of all material conditions on ATM referral advertisements
- Remediation for affected customers
- Assessment of administrative monetary penalty up to $10M per count (s.74.01)
