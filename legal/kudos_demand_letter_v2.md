## IV. LEGAL ANALYSIS

### 4.1 Fraudulent Misrepresentation

This is Kudos's most serious legal exposure. Tatiana's recorded admission eliminates the defence of innocent mistake. The manager's false email claim constitutes a second, independent act of fraudulent misrepresentation.

**Elements as settled in Canadian common law (universally accepted by Canadian courts):**

**Representation 1 — Norman's Pricing and Fee Misrepresentations:**
1. **False representation:** $10/month quoted; $70/month billed. No hookup fees; $490 charged. — *Tatiana admission confirms both*
2. **Made knowingly or recklessly:** Norman knew his representations departed from Kudos-authorized pricing and policy — *Tatiana confirmed Norman violated internal policy*
3. **Intent to induce:** Made to induce Mr. Francisco to enter the arrangement despite his express statement he could not afford $70/month — *Established*
4. **Reliance:** Mr. Francisco would not have entered the arrangement at $70/month — *Established*
5. **Damage:** $1,358.21 in unauthorized charges; $60/month ongoing overcharge; medical harm; disruption — *Established*

**Representation 2 — Manager's False Email Claim:**
1. **False representation:** Manager claimed an email notification of the $823.21 withdrawal was sent May 12–14, 2026 — *No such email exists after exhaustive search*
2. **Made knowingly or recklessly:** The manager either knew no email was sent (false) or made the claim without confirming it (reckless) — *Established*
3. **Intent:** Made to deny liability for the $823.21 unauthorized withdrawal — *Established*
4. **Reliance:** If accepted, this false claim would have denied Mr. Francisco his right to dispute the $823.21 charge — *Established*
5. **Damage:** Continued unauthorized charge; delay in resolution — *Established*

**Damages (fraudulent misrepresentation):** $10,000 (encompassing both misrepresentations and flowing consequences)

### 4.2 Negligent Misrepresentation (Alternative)

In the alternative, if a court finds Norman's and the manager's misrepresentations were negligent rather than fraudulent:

**Elements per *Queen v. Cognos Inc.*, [1993] 1 SCR 87:**

1. Special relationship — Kudos agent-customer relationship — **established**
2. Representation untrue/misleading — $10/month vs. $70/month; free hookup vs. $490; non-existent email notification — **established**
3. Representor acted negligently — Norman failed to verify authorized pricing; manager failed to confirm email was sent before asserting it — **established**
4. Reasonable reliance — Mr. Francisco relied on all three representations — **established**
5. Detriment — $1,358.21 unauthorized charges; ongoing harm — **established**

**Damages (negligent misrepresentation, alternative):** $10,000

### 4.3 Conversion — $1,358.21 Total Unauthorized Charges

Kudos took **$1,358.21** from Mr. Francisco without authorization or colour of right:
- $823.21 — withdrawal without valid notification
- $490.00 — hookup fees contrary to agreement (Tatiana admission)
- $45.00 — unauthorized debit (Tatiana admission)

Tatiana's June 16, 2026 admissions confirm the $490 and $45 charges were taken without authorization. The $823.21 charge lacks valid notification. All three constitute conversion.

**Damages:** $1,358.21 (full refund) + interest from date of taking

### 4.4 Negligence

Kudos owed Mr. Francisco a duty of care as a customer (*Cooper v. Hobart*, 2001 SCC 79). Norman's pricing misrepresentations, violation of internal policy, the manager's false email claim, and the 24+ hour failure to resolve without proper escalation constitute breach at multiple levels.

**Damages:** $2,500 (time, inconvenience, consequential harm from Kudos's systemic failure)

### 4.5 Breach of Contract

The arrangement with Norman constitutes a contract: $10/month per device, no hookup fees, delivery of 5 smartwatches and 2 tablets. Kudos breached:
- Billing $70/month instead of $10/month
- Charging $490 in hookup fees instead of zero
- Failing to deliver promised devices (if undelivered)
- Taking $823.21 without proper notification

**Damages:** $1,358.21 (unauthorized charges) + $60/month × months of service (price differential) + $3,000 (devices, if not delivered)

### 4.6 Disability Discrimination — Ontario Human Rights Code

Sections 5 and 11 of the *Ontario Human Rights Code* prohibit discrimination in services on the basis of disability and impose a duty to accommodate.

Norman required Mr. Francisco to attend in person at a shopping mall while disabled, without offering any accessible alternative for identity verification or stock inspection. This is a failure to accommodate under s.11 of the Code.

The pattern of 24+ hours of unresolved customer service — across four representatives (Norman, Tatiana, Glen, Sherry) — without any disability accommodation discussion, constitutes systemic failure under s.5.

**Note:** HRTO application to be filed concurrently. This letter does not waive HRTO rights.

**Damages:** $5,000 (injury to dignity, feelings, and self-respect; HRTO application filed concurrently)

### 4.7 Medical Aggravation — Thin Skull Principle

Per *Athey v. Leonati*, [1996] 3 SCR 458, Kudos takes Mr. Francisco as it finds him. The 24+ hours of unresolved calls, left without food and medicine, aggravated Mr. Francisco's pre-existing medical conditions.

**Damages:** $2,500 (with supporting physician letter)

### 4.8 Punitive Damages

*Whiten v. Pilot Insurance*, 2002 SCC 18: punitive damages are appropriate where conduct is "harsh, vindictive, reprehensible, and malicious" and compensatory damages alone are insufficient.

Kudos's conduct warrants punitive damages because:
- Norman made specific misrepresentations knowing they exceeded his authority (Tatiana's admission)
- The manager made a false claim about an email notification that does not exist — a fabricated defence
- Management (Glen, Sherry) abandoned Mr. Francisco after undertaking to call back within 48 hours
- The pattern spans multiple representatives over multiple weeks with no resolution

The manager's false email claim is particularly egregious: it is a fabricated post-incident misrepresentation designed to deny Mr. Francisco a legitimate complaint. This is not a training failure — it is deliberate conduct.

**Punitive damages claimed:** $25,000
